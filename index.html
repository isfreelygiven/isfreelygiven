What Freely Given does.

Make consent easier to understand, easier to talk about, and easier to defend in public life.

01 Culture Change

Move consent from a private conversation to a public standard.

Through awareness, language, and sustained cultural attention — making consent something people expect, not just hope for.

02 Accountability Tools

Build resources that support safety and informed choices.

Public-interest tools that bring visibility, structure, and accountability to issues too often ignored.

03 Awareness Campaigns

Keep consent visible in culture, media, and community life.

Campaigns that widen awareness, invite participation, and keep the conversation going beyond a single headline.

The work so far

Two flagship initiatives — a public accountability tool and an annual awareness campaign — with more to come.

Accountability Tool

badactoridentifier.com

A public-facing accountability tool under the Freely Given umbrella, built to support safer, more informed choices where trust and safety matter.

  • Part of a wider nonprofit mission focused on consent, safety, and public awareness.
  • A practical entry point into the accountability side of the work.
  • Sits alongside education and advocacy as one piece of a larger system.

Annual Campaign

International Consent Day

Observed every August 1 — a clear public moment to talk about consent, attraction, respect, and what culture change actually looks like.

  • An annual focal point for supporters, educators, and advocates.
  • 2026 theme: "Consent is sexy" — with the campaign line "A sexy summer starts with consent."
  • Built to grow from a single day into Consent Week.
