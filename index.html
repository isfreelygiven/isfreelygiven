Because "I didn't know" shouldn't be an excuse. And "I was afraid to say no" shouldn't be a story anyone has to tell.

Freely Given is building the infrastructure for a world where respect is the default — not the exception. Where people have the language, the tools, and the cultural backing to set boundaries and be heard.

How we get there.

Three pillars. One mission: make respect, safety, and informed choice the public standard — not a private hope.

01 Culture Change

Make respect as obvious as seatbelts.

Seatbelts went from optional to obvious. We're doing the same for boundaries — through language, visibility, and sustained public attention until it's just how things work.

02 Accountability Tools

Check before you trust.

Starting with badactoridentifier.com — a tool that helps you look someone up before you meet them, flag patterns, and make safer choices with better information.

03 Awareness Campaigns

Give the conversation a date on the calendar.

International Consent Day, August 1. A recurring public moment that keeps the conversation going beyond any single headline — growing toward Consent Week.

Two flagship initiatives

A public accountability tool and an annual awareness campaign — the first two pieces of a larger system.

Accountability Tool

badactoridentifier.com

A public resource for people who want to make more informed decisions about who they trust — in dating, business, or daily life.

Annual Campaign

International Consent Day

Every August 1 — a clear public moment to talk about boundaries, attraction, respect, and what it actually takes to change a culture.

  • 2026 theme: "Consent is sexy" — shareable, bold, designed to spread.
  • An annual rallying point for advocates, educators, and anyone who cares.
  • Built to grow from a single day into Consent Week.
